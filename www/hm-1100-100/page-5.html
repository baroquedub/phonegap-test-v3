The marketing research process

As you can see, marketing research is very like conducting clinical research!

  • Define the problem
  • Define the research objectives
  • Choose data sources
  • Choose appropriate research methods
  • Set budget and deadline
  • Construct sample
  • Undertake research
  • Analyse and evaluate results

The limitations of marketing research also have a great deal in common with clinical research. They include:

  • Problems relating to survey and sampling methods
  • Sampling errors
  • Non-response errors
  • Data collection errors
  • Analytical and reporting errors
  • Timeliness

Your Task

Identify the key characteristics for each of the following examples of pharmaceutical marketing research.

Are they:

  1. Qualitative or quantitative?
  2. Ad hoc or continuous?
  3. If quantitative – is it desk based or field based?
  4. If field based – is it experimental, observational or survey?
Scenario 1 A 'detail follow up' survey (known in the industry as a 'DFU') where doctors who have been detailed (promoted to) by a representative are identified. If they consent to take part in the research they are then telephoned by a researcher who uses a structured questionnaire to discuss their perceptions of the product and recollections of the call. This form of research is undertaken to measure the effectiveness of the promotional materials and sales call.
Scenario 2 A focus group of 10 family doctors who manage patients with diabetes. The research will explore how they decide when to move a patient onto insulin from oral treatments and how they feel about this with the aim of understanding the type of educational support the company (an insulin manufacturer) might provide in this area.
Scenario 3 A retained group of oncologists who complete a quarterly on-line questionnaire about the number of patients with particular tumour types that they have seen in the preceding period.
Scenario 4 A study in which people are invited to the company offices where they are asked to open three potential new forms of packaging for a soluble over the counter painkiller and complete an assessment form rating the ease of opening each one.
