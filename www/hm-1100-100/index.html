The Principles of Marketing Research

Market research - doesn't the name tell you everything you need to know? Just as clinical research is about observing and measuring patients, so market research is about observing and measuring markets? Well yes. But market research – the thing that market researchers do - is actually about quite a bit more than that. Some market researchers like to distinguish market research from marketing research.

Image courtesy of cooldesign / FreeDigitalPhotos.net

Marketing research can be defined as: the collection and analysis of data from a sample of individuals or organisations relating to their characteristics, behaviour, attitudes, opinions or possessions. It includes all forms of marketing, social and opinion research, such as consumer and industrial surveys, psychological investigations, observational and panel studies. It can cover product testing research, promotional research, price research and research on competitors. Marketing research is undertaken to assist decision making in marketing.

In contrast, market research has a narrower scope – the acquisition of data about customers and customer attitudes and the process of identifying information about demand for a product.

We'll try to remember and stick to 'marketing research' from now on!

Image courtesy of Scott Chan / FreeDigitalPhotos.net

So what is the purpose of marketing research? Why do people do it? There are a number of reasons:

  • To understand markets
  • To identify changes in the market
  • To understand customer needs
  • To reduce risk and uncertainty
  • To anticipate/forecast market trends
  • To provide a sound basis for marketing decisions
  • To support marketing planning
