Qualitative and Quantitative Research

One of the key distinctions in marketing research is between Qualitative and Quantitative research.

Quantitative research is about making measurements. The aim is to product hard, statistically valid numerical data â€“ in that respect it has a lot in common with a great deal of clinical research. Quantitative marketing research is based on using representative samples to determine parameters such as sales volume and revenue, market share and market growth.

Image courtesy of cooldesign / FreeDigitalPhotos.net

Qualitative research focuses on the values, attitudes, opinions, beliefs and motivations of customers. It's concerned with the psychology of buying decisions and aims to understand customer behaviour, attitudes and perceptions. It is often impressionistic rather than conclusive and represents a way of identifying reasons behind the trends. Qualitative research uses smaller samples but with the aim of gaining a greater depth of information. It uses techniques such as in depth interviews or focus groups.
