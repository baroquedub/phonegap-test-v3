Market research

Some market researchers talk about the seven O's of market research – its just a way of listing the aspects of a market that can be examined.

  • Occupants - who constitutes the market?
  • Object – what do they buy?
  • Objectives - why do they buy?
  • Organisations - who participates in the buying decisions?
  • Operations - how do they buy?
  • Occasions - when do they buy?
  • Outlets - where do they buy?

Image courtesy of jscreationzs / FreeDigitalPhotos.net

The aim of market research is to get information about the market for a particular product, to understand the likely demand and trends.

Other aspects of marketing research include

  • Product research
  • Price research
  • Promotional research
  • Distribution research
  • Information about the product and how it is used
  • Competing products
  • Customer acceptance
  • Test marketing
  • Potential new users/uses
  • Customer perceptions of price/quality/value
  • Price sensitivity – price elasticity of demand
  • Profit margins
  • Effects of advertising on sales
  • Effectiveness of promotional methods
  • Effectiveness of different media
  • Choice of distribution channels
  • Choice of outlets
  • Dealer requirements
  • Packaging requirements
  • Transport and storage costs
